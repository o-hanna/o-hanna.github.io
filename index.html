About Me

I am a Research Scientist at Meta Superintelligence Lab (MSL) in Menlo Park. My current work focuses on Post-training and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) for large language models, including leading efforts for post-training the audio Llama model family.

I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UCLA (2024), advised by Prof. Christina Fragouli and Prof. Lin F. Yang. My doctoral research on online learning and information theory was recognized with the UCLA Distinguished Ph.D. Dissertation Research Award (2024-2025).

My background is in mathematics and theoretical engineering. During my time at UCLA, I dove deep into graduate-level optimization, probability theory, and mathematical logic (including Gödel’s theorems and axiomatic set theory); early in my journey, I ranked 1st in the Egyptian Math Olympiad. Today, I enjoy connecting those theoretical concepts to practical, large-scale AI alignment and efficiency problems.
